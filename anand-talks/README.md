On February 17, 2011, S. Anand created a GitHub repository called "talks." After three commits, it went dormant for **5,112 days**. Then, on **May 20, 2025**, a single commit changed everything:

> "Revamp and add new vibe coding talk transcript"

What followed was an explosion of productivity: **29 talks in 7 months**. A **2,800% increase** in documented output.

This data story investigates:
- What triggered the May 2025 transformation?
- How did "Vibe Coding" enable its own documentation?
- What does a productivity explosion look like when the cost of knowledge synthesis drops to near-zero?
